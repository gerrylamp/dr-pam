When God Says Wait

Posted on April 30, 2025 · By Dr. Pamela Russell

Waiting is one of the hardest things we’re asked to do in the walk of faith. When God gives us a promise, shows us a glimpse of purpose, or speaks a word into our spirit — we want it *now*.

But there’s divine purpose in delay.

“Though it tarry, wait for it…” — Habakkuk 2:3

When God says wait, He’s not withholding — He’s preparing. Maturity. Clarity. Protection. The delay isn’t denial. It’s alignment. God may be aligning you, the people you’re meant to serve, or the very environment where the purpose will bloom.

Why Waiting is Holy

  • It deepens your trust in God, not your own timeline
  • It detaches you from performance-based worth
  • It protects you from premature exposure or burnout

God’s silence doesn’t mean absence. Keep preparing. Keep praying. Keep moving in obedience, even when you’re not yet moving in visibility.

How to Wait Well

Here’s how I’ve learned to wait (and it’s still a work in progress):

  1. Stay in the Word — let truth speak louder than the clock
  2. Practice gratitude — even in the gaps
  3. Ask, “What can I steward now?”

Your promise is not forgotten. Your preparation is not wasted. And your delay is not without design.

You’re not behind. You’re just in God's process.
